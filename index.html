Vanessa Qian

Hi! I'm a fourth-year undergraduate at UC Berkeley studying history and computer science. I care about telling clear and meaningful stories with data, research, and code. Areas of interest include China, all things tech, and education.

Portfolio

China Unbound

For NPR International Desk's series on China's global reach, the graphics visualize the extent of SOEs' stakes in European and Mediterranean ports and Chinese investments in major sectors in Africa, debt in Kenya, and worldwide over time.

The New Housing Crisis: Shut Out Of The Market

The curtain raiser for NPR's August 2018 homeownership series featured graphics that spoke to reasons, including construction lags and lack of affordable housing, underlying America's homeownership crisis. In this crisis, while homeownership rates since 1987 have by and large improved for other major minorities, black homeownership has fallen steadily behind.

Drowning In Plastic Trash

In this project with NPR's Science Desk, the graphic converts massive numbers representing the collective responsibility for plastic trash on land and in the ocean into numbers that tell of individual responsibility for the mountains of plastic waste that ends up in our oceans and on land.

The School Shootings That Weren't

A collaboration with NPR's Education desk, the visual seeks to explain the confusion surrounding school shooting data.

Mapgenbot: A Slackbot Integration

At Yik Yak, polls were a key form of user engagement - and a key source of data about the largely millennial user base. Making use of polls and geographic data, maps of opinions about the EU Referendum and sentiment right before the California primary are a couple of the many maps that told current events from Yik Yak users' perspectives. To streamline the map making process, I built a pipeline that took care of all the steps from data querying to visualization. I integrated the pipeline into a slackbot, Mapgenbot, that responded to simple direct messages that fed it a poll and some options for personalization (e.g. legend, colors).

Mapping food mirages

Food mirages are communities that suffer from similar symptoms as communities that classifed by the USDA as food deserts do. Examining factors related to gentrification over time, the project sought to identify food mirages in San Francisco.
