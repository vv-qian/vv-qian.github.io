The Covid Storm

To achieve a "floating field of objects" effect, I used CSS's z-axis — a first for me! I tinkered around with Javascript libraries such as Rellax and, after coming across this post on performant parallaxing, a purely CSS parallax approach. The CSS-only approach leverages how objects placed at different points on the z-axis appear when scrolling. In the end, I opted for the CSS approach because I liked its simplicity and novelty. Although I experienced a steep learning curve understanding how perspective and translateZ work together, this project was full of fun experimentation.

The Month Coronavirus Felled American Business

To immediately convey the sudden rise of coronavirus cases in the U.S. and the rapidity with which the American business landscape was forced to deal with the pandemic, we used a full-screen, scroll-driven bar chart representing the number of confirmed cases as an impactful introduction to the story.

Dotting the story were calendrical subheads, or guide posts, to help the reader keep track of key economic and virus metrics as the story progressed chronologically.

The Credit Score Game

The idea of gamifying the project came up early in the discussion as an effective and engaging way for readers to gauge their credit-score knowledge and to demystify the "whys" behind credit-score dips and rises. We needed to cater to as broad of a reader population as possible, while preserving enough profile specificity to be able to offer helpful information. In the end, we offered five profiles with scores across the spectrum and a unique set of questions and responses for each.

This project was several firsts for me — first time using Vue.js, first time in complete control of project design and management and first time collaborating with another newsroom developer.

A Decade of News: How the Big Stories Evolved

The result of a WSJ analysis of articles published in the 2010s was the star of the project. To highlight and augment the narrative arc in each topic analysis, I decided to let the user's scrolling drive when phrases were highlighted and when context was provided. The simple column animation provides a sense of the narratives unfolding as the years progress. I developed this in Vue.js — after using the framework for the first time in "The Credit-Score Game," I found every chance to leverage its responsiveness.

In Valuing Colleges, Tops in Prestige Doesn’t Always Mean Tops in Starting Salaries

The visualization's triangular design — coded in d3.js — functions as an overview of earnings distribution and rankings and offers a look at how prestige, or selectivity, impacts earnings. It's a multifunctional design that I hope to use again.
